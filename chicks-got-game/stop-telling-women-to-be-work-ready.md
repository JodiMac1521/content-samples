# Stop Telling Women to Be "Work-Ready"

**Category:** Chicks Got Game
**Author:** Jodi Mac — Founder & CEO, Work Simplr
**Published:** March 20, 2026
**Originally published on:** [LinkedIn Pulse](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/stop-telling-women-work-ready-jodi-mcpherson-xcvjc)

---

*The system is outdated. The advice is worse.*

---

Let's get something straight.

Women are not "unprepared" for work. The system is unprepared for them.

And the longer we keep pretending otherwise — telling women to "build their confidence," "polish their CV," "wait until they're ready" — the longer we trap them in a game that's already been rewritten.

---

## The biggest lie? That readiness comes first.

We've been sold a sequence:

**Learn → Prepare → Get picked → Start**

Logical. Completely broken.

Because here's what's actually happening:

- Entry-level roles are disappearing
- Internships are paywalled or network-gated
- AI is eating the very tasks that used to train you

So tell me — how exactly is someone supposed to get "ready" for work… when the starting line keeps moving?

We didn't just raise the bar. We removed the door.

---

## Women don't have a confidence problem. They have an access problem.

I'm going to say the quiet part out loud.

The "confidence gap" narrative? Convenient. It puts the burden on women instead of the system.

*"Just apply anyway." "Back yourself more." "Lean in."*

Cool. To what, exactly?

You can't confidence your way into opportunities that don't exist or aren't accessible.

What we're actually seeing is an **opportunity gap:**

- Do the "right" things → still no entry point
- Follow the rules → still no seat at the table
- Get qualified → still told "not enough experience"

It's not a pipeline problem. It's a broken on-ramp.

---

## AI didn't break the system. It exposed it.

Everyone's panicking about AI like it just showed up and wrecked the place. No. It turned the lights on.

The jobs that used to train people? Gone or shrinking. The tasks that filled early careers? Automated or commoditized.

What's left?

- Thinking
- Judgment
- Context
- Voice

The problem? You don't learn those by watching. You learn them by doing.

And we've made "doing" harder to access than ever.

---

## Internships are not the answer anymore

Let's stop pretending internships are still the golden bridge. For a lot of women, they're:

- Unpaid
- In cities they don't live in
- Dependent on who you know
- Logistically impossible

That's not a pathway. That's a filter.

And guess who it filters out first? Not the least capable. The least resourced.

---

## The market doesn't reward preparation. It rewards proof.

Here's the shift nobody is saying clearly enough:

No one cares what you *could* do. They care what you've *already* done.

Not your degree. Not your course. Not your intentions.

**Your receipts.**

What have you built? What have you written? What problem have you actually solved?

This is brutal if you're waiting for permission. And freeing if you stop.

---

## The new game: build before you're chosen

The old model said: **Get picked → then produce.**

The new reality is: **Produce → then get picked.**

That's the inversion. And it changes everything.

For writers, especially — stop waiting for someone to commission you. Write the damn piece. Publish it. Do it again.

Not into the void — into something real:

- A specific audience
- A real question
- A point of view that actually risks disagreement

Because "good writing" isn't rare anymore. Clear thinking is.

---

## Your portfolio is your power now

Forget the perfect CV for a second. Your real leverage? A body of work that proves:

- You can think
- You can execute
- You can finish

Not one piece. Not a draft folder. Not "I'm working on it."

Visible. Consistent. Real.

This is where most people stall — not because they can't do it, but because no one told them this counts.

**It does. It counts more than you think.**

---

## Let's redefine "experience" (because the old definition is useless)

Experience is **not:**
- Time spent waiting
- Titles you didn't get
- Gatekeepers you couldn't access

Experience **is:**
- Work you initiated
- Problems you engaged with
- Output you can point to

A self-started series? Experience. A collaboration with a small org? Experience. Writing consistently about something that matters? Experience.

Stop disqualifying your own work because it didn't come with a logo.

---

## "Work-ready" is the wrong goal

Here's the part that might sting.

"Work-ready" isn't just outdated — it's passive. It assumes:

- Someone else defines readiness
- Someone else grants access
- Someone else decides when you begin

No thanks.

A better question: **Are you in motion?**

Because the people who win in this shift aren't the most prepared. They're the ones who:

- Start before they feel ready
- Stay in the work longer than others
- Build proof while others build plans

---

## We are in a weird, in-between moment.

Old rules still being taught. New rules already in play.

Institutions? Slow. Advice? Outdated. Gatekeepers? Still guarding doors that matter less by the day.

And women? Still being told to wait.

Don't.

---

## Final thought: readiness is earned in motion

You don't become ready and then begin.

**You begin — and readiness catches up.**

Messy. Imperfect. Visible. That's the work now.

And yeah — it's uncomfortable. But so is sitting on the sidelines, perfectly prepared for a version of the world that no longer exists.
